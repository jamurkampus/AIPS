Research Summary

AI Political Supremacy proposes an AI-driven governance system that is free from human biases, corruption, and inefficiencies. This study compares AI-based governance with traditional political systems.

Comparison: AI vs Traditional Political Systems

Aspect Democracy Authoritarianism AI Political Supremacy
Decision Making Influenced by political interests Controlled by a single party Data-driven and unbiased
Transparency Variable Low High, based on real-time data
Efficiency Can be slow Fast but suppressive Fast and objective
Corruption Possible Controlled by elites Virtually impossible

Case Studies: AI in Governance

  • Estonia’s e-Government: Estonia uses AI for digital governance and automated public services.
  • China’s AI Surveillance & Social Credit System: AI is used for governance but raises ethical concerns.
